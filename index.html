Research

Prime Labelings of Generalized Petersen Graphs

December 2014 - December 2015

Presented at Mathematical Association of America 2015 Conference and awarded Second Place Student Paper.

Published in Involve Vol. 10 (2017), No. 1, 109-124.

Abstract

A graph G is called prime if the vertices of G signed distinct labels 1,2,...,|V(G)| such that the labels on any two adjacent vertices are relatively prime. By showing that for every even n ≤ 2.468x10^9 there exists s ∈ [1,n-1] such that both n+s and 2n+s are prime, we prove the generalized Peterson graph P(n,1) is prime for all even n ∈ [4,2.468x10^9].
